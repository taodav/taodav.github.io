Stir-fry eggs with tomatoes (西红柿炒鸡蛋)

Prep: 10 minutes

Cooking: ~10 minutes, depending on how saucy you want your dish

Serves 4 (Adjust accordingly! You could add more tomatoes/more eggs depending on what you're feeling)

Eggs stir-fried with tomatoes. As simple as Chinese dishes get! This is one of those dishes where ingredient quantity/cooking methods depend a lot on personal preference. So adjust according to how you like it! The recipe here is how I've always made it and how my 阿姨 used to make it back at home!

Method

1. Chop the tomatoes and garlic. For the tomatoes I chopped them into eighths and removed the stemmy portion. You could chop these smaller or keep them larger, depending on how you want your tomatoes in your final dish (do you want mushier tomatoes (smaller) or more solid pieces (larger)). Chop and dice garlic.

2. Beat your eggs in a mixing bowl. Salt your eggs lightly here.

3. Add oil to your wok/pan and fry your eggs over medium heat. Keep pushing the cooked eggs around so that the uncooked eggs make contact with the bottom of the pan. I like to keep my eggs just slightly runny when I remove it from heat. Transfer to a plate for use later.

4. Oil your wok/pan once again and fry your chopped garlic until fragrant under medium heat, about 1 minute. Add your chopped tomatoes and incorporate with the garlic. Add a cup of water and allow the wok/pan to come to a simmer. I like to add water to this part because it lets the tomatoes cook down even more and results in a saucier/soupier dish at the end. Reduce the water down to the desired consistency.

5. Add the sugar and fried eggs into the wok/pan. Incorporate both and add a dash of light soy sauce to the wok/pan. Stir again gently, so that you don't break apart your eggs too much. Finally, salt to taste and serve!

6. Enjoy on some rice! I made this along with some spinach and fried halloumi. I know this sounds weird but I eat fried halloumi with everything!

Ingredients
  • Tomatoes - 4 medium-sized
  • Large eggs - 6
  • Garlic - 3 to 4 cloves
  • Cooking oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Unrefined sugar - 1 1/2 tablespoons (other sugars work too!)
  • Water - 1 cup (optional)
  • Salt - to taste
  • Light soy sauce - a dash (optional)
Tools
  • Frying pan
  • Mixing bowl
  • Cutting board + knife
